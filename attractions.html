Ukrainian Points of Interest

Ukraine is vast and varied in its cities and nature, with something for everyone. Have a look at the main attractions of Ukraine!

Lviv

Lviv is a historic city in western Ukraine, known for its cobblestone streets, baroque and neoclassical architecture, and vibrant cultural life. It is often called the cultural capital of Ukraine, with numerous theaters, museums, and coffee houses. The cityâ€™s historic center is a UNESCO World Heritage Site, attracting visitors from around the world.

Chernobyl

Chernobyl is a town in northern Ukraine, best known for the catastrophic nuclear disaster that occurred at the nearby power plant in 1986. The area was evacuated and remains part of an exclusion zone due to lingering radiation. Today, it serves as a site of scientific study and historical tourism, symbolizing both tragedy and resilience.

The Carpathian Mountains

The Carpathian Mountains stretch across western Ukraine, offering stunning landscapes of forests, meadows, and rolling peaks. They are home to diverse wildlife, traditional Hutsul culture, and picturesque wooden churches. The region is popular for hiking, skiing, and eco-tourism, attracting visitors year-round.
